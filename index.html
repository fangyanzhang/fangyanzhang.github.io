Research

Large-Scale Graphs Visualization

Mississippi State University

Designed and developed a scalable large-scale graph visualization tool-BGS (Big Graph Surfer) to visualize graphs with billion-scale vertices/edges. This system creates hierarchical structure from original graphs by using graph-based machine learning algorithms and provide interactive navigation along the hierarchy by expanding or collapsing clusters when visualizing large-scale graphs.

August 2016 - October 2017

Distributed Graph Sampling Techniques

Mississippi State University

Designed and developed a distributed large-scale graph sampling package on GraphX, which bring a great improvement on sampling efficiency without losing sampling accuracy and increases sampling efficiency by a factor of more than a hundred when compared to traditional traversal-based sampling.

August 2016 - May 2017

Large-Scale Graph Simulation

Mississippi State University

Developed a graph modeling/fitting algorithm which can simulate/scale up existing graphs to large-scale graphs with billion-scale vertices/edges while preserving graph properties.

August 2015 - January 2017

Graph Sampling for Visual Analytics

Mississippi State University

Evaluated commonly used graph sampling methods through a combined visual and statistical comparison of graphs sampled at various rates and built a benchmark.

August 2014 - May 2016

Ensemble Data Analytics and Visualization

Mississippi State University

Applied weather data to 2D and 3D scale-space and developed several techniques to remove small features and highlight dominant features at a high scale in visualization.

August 2014 - May 2016

Real-time Soft Shadow Rendering

Capital Normal University & Graduate University of Chinese Academy of Science

Designed and implemented real-time soft shadow in 3D Desktop System-Space, which makes the Space system looks more realistic.

June 2010 - May 2012
